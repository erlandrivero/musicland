## Pro Tips for Better Results

### 1. **Be Specific with Tempo**
Always include BPM (beats per minute) for consistent results:
- Slow: 60-80 BPM
- Medium: 90-120 BPM
- Fast: 130-180 BPM

### 2. **Layer Your Descriptions**
Combine multiple elements:
- Rhythm (drums, percussion)
- Melody (synths, instruments)
- Atmosphere (mood, vibe)
- Energy (calm, energetic, aggressive)

### 3. **Use Genre-Specific Terms**
Each genre has its own vocabulary:
- Hip-Hop: 808s, hi-hats, boom bap
- EDM: drops, build-ups, wobble bass
- Rock: distortion, power chords, riffs

### 4. **Describe the Mood**
Include emotional descriptors:
- Happy, sad, energetic, calm
- Dark, bright, mysterious, uplifting
- Aggressive, gentle, powerful, subtle

### 5. **Reference Eras or Styles**
Add time period or style references:
- "80s synth-pop"
- "90s boom bap"
- "Modern trap"
- "Classic rock"

### 6. **Experiment with Combinations**
Mix genres for unique sounds:
- "Jazz-influenced hip-hop"
- "Electronic rock fusion"
- "Orchestral EDM"

### 7. **Keep It Under 400 Characters**
AI works best with concise, focused prompts. If you need more detail, prioritize the most important elements.

### 8. **Use Action Words**
Describe what the music does:
- "Builds gradually"
- "Drops hard"
- "Flows smoothly"
- "Pulses energetically"
